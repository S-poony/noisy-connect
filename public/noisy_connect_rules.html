Noisy Connect — how to play

Noisy Connect

A game of precision under uncertainty

The setup

There is a hidden sine wave. You don't know its amplitude or frequency. Each turn, you choose an x value and place a piece on the wave. But two things go wrong:

  • Your shaky hand adds a small random wobble η to your x — so the piece lands somewhere slightly off.
  • Your blurry eyes add a noise ε to what you see — the y value shown (grey dot) is not the true value.
What you see vs. what is real

Grey dots — what you observe each turn (noisy). This is your only feedback while playing.

Blue dots — the true outputs. Hidden until you claim victory.

How to win

Claim victory when you believe you have a Connect 4: at least 4 true outputs (blue dots) that satisfy both conditions:

1. Clustered — all 4 fit inside a y-axis window of height 4.
2. Spread out — no two of the 4 are closer than 0.5 to each other.
How to lose

Claim victory too soon and the blue dots may not form a valid Connect 4. Any dots that violate the spacing rule (too close together) are shown in red.
